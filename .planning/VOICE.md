# VOICE.md — Numen Landing Voice Baseline

> **Canonical source of truth for every word on the Numen landing.**
> Phases 2–4 read this before writing ANY copy — heading, subhead, button label,
> microcopy, alt text, or metadata. Authored in Phase 1 (D-08). If a line can't be
> self-checked against this doc in seconds, the doc has failed — keep it scannable.

---

## The Register

Calm, plain-language, **confident-mentor**. You are the sharp friend who has
watched a thousand videos and tells you the truth — kindly, specifically, without
flattery or fear. Never breathless. Never salesy. Never a hype machine.

- **Second person, always.** "your content", "your video", "you" — not "users",
  not "creators" in body copy, not "our AI".
- **Confident, not loud.** State the value plainly and stop. No exclamation points,
  no "🚀", no "game-changer", no "revolutionary".
- **Specificity over abstraction (luma discipline).** "reads your video like your
  sharpest audience would" beats "powered by advanced intelligence". Concrete beats
  grand every time.
- **Honest by construction.** The product's whole moat is that it does not lie to
  you. The copy must never lie to you either — no inflated promise the engine can't
  keep.

---

## Hard Rule 1 — ZERO engine jargon

The visitor never sees how the engine works. Forbidden anywhere on the page:

- "Apollo", "fold", "Omni", "model", "pipeline", "LLM", "inference"
- "score machinery", "weighted sum", "calibration layer", "embeddings"
- any internal system name or architecture term

The reader cares what they get, not how it's built. Talk about *their* video and
*their* verdict — never about *our* machinery.

## Hard Rule 2 — ZERO hype / fake precision (the anti-snake-oil moat)

This is the positioning moat. It is not a section — it lives in every line.
**Forbidden anywhere on the page:**

- "X% accuracy", "95% accurate", any naked percentage promise
- "predict virality", "go viral", "guaranteed", "guaranteed views"
- "AI-powered predictions", "viral score", "virality score"
- any naked metric promise or fake-precision number used as a hook

The rivals sell a fake "virality score". Numen sells an **honest verdict**. The
copy must read as the antidote to snake oil, never as more of it. (The old root
metadata — "Know what will go viral before you post", "AI-powered predictions" —
was a textbook violation and was removed in Phase 1.)

## Hard Rule 3 — Verdict = band + one-line why, never a naked number

Whenever copy refers to what Numen returns: it is always a **calibrated band**
(e.g. "this will likely land" / "this needs work") **plus one line of WHY**.
**Never** present the verdict as a bare number or score. This binds all Phase 2–3
verdict copy. A number with no reasoning is exactly the snake oil we reject.

## Do-words / Don't-words

| Do (write these) | Don't (never these) |
|------------------|---------------------|
| honest verdict | viral score / virality score |
| reads your video | predicts / AI-powered prediction |
| will it land | will it go viral |
| your sharpest audience | our advanced AI |
| act on / what to fix | guaranteed / guaranteed views |
| before you post | before you blow up |
| calibrated band + why | 95% accuracy / X% accuracy |
| clear, plain, specific | game-changer, revolutionary, unlock |
| you, your content | users, creators (in body copy) |
| confident and calm | breathless, salesy, urgent |

## Self-check (run on every line before shipping)

1. Second person? Is it about *their* content, not *our* tech?
2. Any engine jargon? (Rule 1) → cut it.
3. Any hype / fake-precision / naked number? (Rule 2) → cut it.
4. If it's a verdict reference: band + why, never a bare number? (Rule 3)
5. Specific over abstract? Could a snake-oil rival have written this line? If yes,
   it's wrong — rewrite until only Numen could have said it.
