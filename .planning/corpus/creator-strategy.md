## 1. How the algorithm actually works  *(Kallaway)*
The platform is a matchmaker optimizing for time-on-app. Posting a video:
1. **Digital fingerprint / topic mapping** — multimodal (vision + audio + metadata) → what the video is about.
2. **Fit score** — prediction of who will like it.
3. **~200-person sample test** — mostly *non-followers* (it tests whether strangers react). Followers barely matter in sampling.
4. **Boost / retry / stop** — positive sample → 200 → 2k → 20k → 200k…; neutral → re-sample; negative → throttle fast. Even mega-bangers fade when they exhaust the interested pool.

**The only two levers:** (a) help it build a *better fit score* → narrow topic + consistent avatar (audience matching); (b) make the sample *engage* → the four video-quality drivers (relevant pain-point · non-obvious + implementable · high absorption · short distance to act). "Posting time, hashtags, captions don't matter. The cake is great videos for a specific avatar across a narrow topic band, over and over."

**Comment-driving tactics (channel-level engagement):** take a clear (often contrarian) stance · pick the contrarian side · amplify the framing · build on cult-loved brands/people · drive emotion.

## 2. The 7 deadly pitfalls  *(Kallaway)*
1. **Paid ads on a working organic account** — boosting from the golden-goose account dilutes the audience avatar + may trigger throttling. Run paid from a *separate* account, never the organic one.
2. **Failure by suffocation** — spreading to many platforms before dominating one. Win one platform + format first; build a system that runs 30 days unbroken before expanding.
3. **Shotgun topics** — covering too many topics confuses the algorithm's cluster. One world-class topic per channel (or separate channels).
4. **Wrong niche** — use the 4-question framework (§3).
5/6. **Visual format** — don't force a signature format on day one (let it emerge from experiments); once you have a winner, don't abandon it.
7. **Ignoring data** — replicate what already works in-niche instead of guessing from the gut.

## 3. Niche & positioning
**Kallaway's 4-question niche framework:** (1) monetizing or not? no → pick what you love. (2) already have a product/brand? yes → that's your niche. (3) beginner (<30 videos in format)? yes → make 20–30 on any loved topic to close the skill gap, expect to maybe restart. (4) monetize via your own products vs brand deals? products → pick where you have unique insight or an audience you'll serve for a decade; brand deals → pick a niche where big companies already pay influencers well.

**5 premium-brand attributes (AI era):** (1) **unique positioning** — at least one of five pillars: premium packaging · originality · tactical usefulness · rare combination · aura. (2) **data-driven creativity** — topic from data, take from originality. (3) **violent volume** — dominate one platform, "content minutes" per week as north star. (4) **quality bar** — "best I could do given time+skill," never below. (5) **obsession factor** — genuinely love the topic or it collapses past ~18 months.

**Hoyos — Blue Ocean:** avoid saturated niches; find the underserved cut ("finance for kids"). **Hoyos — content buckets:** 2–4 repeatable formats with *consistent metadata* so the algorithm links them.

## 4. Volume, cadence & ideation
- **Violent volume on one home platform** until dominated, *then* expand (Kallaway). Pick home platform = where your viewers live ∩ format you won't burn out on.
- **Platform cadence (conflict — match the monetization model):** Hormozi 5–10/day (B2B education) · Ava 15–30/mo per client · Hoyos ~1/week, Sat 10am EST (virality). Min 1/week. Never zero, never spam.
- **Hoyos idea funnel:** 1,000 → 100 → 25 → 10. **6-question idea filter:** want to make it? · logistically possible? · strong hook? · good mechanism? · rewatch value? · virality potential?
- **Repurpose winners:** ~70% of creative = variations of top performers (Hormozi 70/20/10: variations / significant variants / new angles). Repost winners with new framing.
- **Hormozi 5 phases:** make → post consistently → post reliably on all platforms (6+ mo) → maximize output → quality+quantity with CTA in every piece. **18 months** min to build momentum.

## 5. Monetization  *(Kallaway 2026 shifts + creators)*
- **Social commerce 3.0** — Kallaway's thesis: the *skill of generating attention* is about to 5–10× in value. Four tailwinds: Instagram Shops (instant affiliate, up to 30 products/video) · agentic social commerce (Meta×Manifold: auto-DM selling, attribution) · falling build costs (more creator-led brands) · click-to-buy computer vision.
- **North-star metric by phase (conflict):** reach phase → retention; growth phase → shareability; monetization phase → RPM / lead conversion. Hormozi: optimize revenue, not views.
- **Value : Ask** — short-form ~98 : 2, long-form 3 : 1 (Hormozi). *(this anchor also lives in the core §2.0a for the per-video close.)*
- **Products vs brand deals** — own products keep more margin but require making *and* selling; brand deals = pick a niche where companies already pay influencers well.
- **Ava's proof of the desire engine:** outlier research → precise hooks → simple formats → friction-free CTA on proof (one video: 7.9M views → ~13k leads).

## 6. Production systems
**Kallaway 6-step content system:** topic (data) → format (data) → substance (creativity: contrarian take + evidence) → hook → script → edit. AI automates everything except the contrarian take ("AI-enabled creativity"). **10x batch system:** run topic/format research once → produce 10 videos → post daily 10 days → look back at conversions/followers/views; any ≥5× winner → run its topic+format 3× in the next batch; ≥10× → run it again exactly.

**Ava production workflow:** Day 1–2 research (100+ in-niche Reels >1M views, apply 5× Rule) → Day 3 write 15–30 scripts on proven hooks + client expertise → Day 4 film 5 talking-head + 5 alt-format → edit & repurpose across platforms. **Impact priority order:** Hook → Script → Format → Editing.

**Hormozi ad assembly:** 6 angles × 5 hooks × 2 = 60 ads. **Repurposing tree:** one long-form → podcast → 2,000-word blog → 5–10 clips → email sequence → LinkedIn carousel. **Twitter = R&D lab** for finding winning angles. **Rule of 100:** 100 actions/day for 100 days.

**Outlier definition (analysis threshold):** ≥5× follower count in views = a genuine outlier worth studying (Ava's 5× Rule); screen sub-2%-engagement high-view videos as likely paid/boosted before treating them as organic winners.
