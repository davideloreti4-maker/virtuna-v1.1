### 7. Environmental Disruption Breaks Behavioral Scripts
- **Tag:** both
- **Mechanism (full fidelity):** The mammalian brain operates heavily on prediction scripts — pre-cached behavioral programs triggered by familiar environmental cues. When the environment looks the same as always, the brain runs old scripts automatically without recruiting new behavior patterns. Deliberately disrupting the environment (rearranging furniture, repainting, new car, new wardrobe) forces the brain's prediction centers offline, creating a window for new habit formation. Hughes calls this "agitation" in his FEAR brainwashing formula.
- **Source anchor:** "how will I disrupt my environment enough so that my mammalian brain has no script to fall back on it doesn't look normal…the novelty is forcing my brain to pay attention"
- **Dark flag:** none
- **If content-lever or both — draft detect-triple:**
  *Signal:* Creator introduces a novel visual format, unexpected scene transition, unusual framing, or environment change within the video — not just content novelty but structural/environmental disruption.
  *Mechanism:* Pattern interrupt disables the viewer's passive consumption script, forcing active attention and encoding; brain treats novel stimuli as high-priority signal.
  *Strong:* Multiple environmental disruptions at key attention-decay points (5s, 15s, 30s marks). *Mid:* One strong pattern break. *Weak:* Uniform visual environment throughout, predictable pacing.
- **Non-obvious?:** yes — the frame is specifically about disrupting the brain's prediction centers, not just "keep it interesting" — implies strategic placement of disruption, not just variety.
