### §2.6.1 — Authority & composure signals *(read off the messenger, mostly multimodal)*
*Kallaway's trust layer (§2.3) covers proof, emulation, and likability. None of it reads the creator's **nervous-system state**. This sub-group does: the audience feels authority before they parse a word, and the tells are physical.*

- **Composure (fear-speed tell)** *(needs video)* — *Signal:* are gestures, head-turns, eye-shifts, and speech pace uniformly *slower* than conversational norm, with no anxious acceleration? · *Mechanism:* fear speeds the body; slowness is the counter-signal the viewer's threat-detection circuit reads as safety and status. Authority sits on a substrate of visible comfort — fidget, jaw/shoulder tension, and "performance strain" demote perceived status before any claim is heard. · *Strong:* sustained calm pace, at ease, camera-irrelevant. *Mid:* mostly calm with isolated speed/tension spikes. *Weak:* fast, fidgety, or visibly straining delivery. *(src: authority-operative-training, dangerous-influence, psyop)*
- **Congruent broadcast (no performance gap)** — *Signal:* does delivery read as grounded, or as "delivering lines"? Do the confident cues (posture, declarative framing) match the emotional affect, or sit on top of it? · *Mechanism:* the limbic system reads incongruence between inner state and words before cognition; authenticity bypasses skepticism, performance triggers it. Editing does not erase this — micro-signals survive cuts and accumulate into a gut feeling the viewer acts on. Authority that looks *effortful* gets downgraded ("acquired"); authority that looks intrinsic reads as character and maximizes trust. · *Strong:* zero hedging/filler, declarative, effortless — confidence looks like who they are. *Mid:* mostly grounded with occasional compensatory energy spikes. *Weak:* scripted cadence, enthusiasm dialed up, visible "trying." *(src: 1pct-control-reality, authority-operative-training, dangerous-influence)*
- **Unshockability** *(needs video)* — *Signal:* does the creator stay unperturbed across provocative, controversial, or difficult material — no visible surprise, defensiveness, or hedging? · *Mechanism:* surprise/fear responses are involuntary and contagious; a creator who reacts transfers the state to the viewer and loses authority. An unshocked creator keeps the viewer in a calm, receptive state. · *Strong:* fully steady across hard material. *Mid:* generally stable, isolated micro-reactions. *Weak:* clear reactive spikes to pushback or difficulty. *(src: authority-operative-training)*
- **Equalizing register (no condescension, no flattery)** — *Signal:* does the creator talk *to* a peer — neither talking down (expert condescension) nor up (parasocial flattery, "you guys are amazing")? · *Mechanism:* viewers are hyper-sensitive to social positioning; a creator who neither dominates nor submits triggers a rare comfort response that extends trust and watch time. Effort-narration ("this took me forever," "please appreciate this") is an active authority *reducer* — it advertises limited capacity. · *Strong:* consistent peer register, no effort-narration. *Mid:* mostly balanced, isolated condescension/flattery/effort spikes. *Weak:* clear talking-down, heavy flattery, or repeated "appreciate my effort" framing. *(src: authority-operative-training)*
- **Perceived-authority container** *(needs video)* — *Signal:* do environment and aesthetic signal institutional legitimacy (studio, clean professional backdrop, consistent expert-channel identity, fluent domain vocabulary) before any claim is evaluated? · *Mechanism:* authority compliance is pre-rational and *environmental*, not intrinsic — a single context cue (the "lab-coat effect") raises the compliance baseline; the brain pattern-matches the container and imports trust without verifying credentials. The container can be as load-bearing as the content. · *Strong:* coherent high-status container, every visual/verbal element consistent. *Mid:* some professional signals, some casual. *Weak:* casual/familiar setting, no authority cue. *(src: influence, compliance, scary-loophole)* · *Caution flag for the reasoner: perceived ≠ real authority — reward the signal's effect on retention, but do not treat container polish as evidence the claim is true (pairs with §5 luck-vs-craft).*
- **Borrowed-schema authority** *(needs video)* — *Signal:* do graphics/format closely mimic a recognized authoritative source (academic diagram, news lower-third, clinical aesthetic, textbook pyramid/color scheme)? · *Mechanism:* the brain auto-imports the credibility attached to a familiar schema; the viewer trusts before consciously evaluating, with no explicit credential claimed. · *Strong:* deliberate, tight match to a known expert format. *Mid:* loose stylistic resemblance to professional/academic content. *Weak:* generic look, no schema borrowing. *(src: scary-loophole)*

### §2.6.2 — Pre-perception & framing *(the category lands before the content)*
*Kallaway grades the hook's content. This sub-group grades the **frame** the hook installs — the category the viewer is placed in before they evaluate anything. "Anything that comes before perception controls perception."*

- **First-frame installation** — *Signal:* does the open declare what *kind* of thing this is ("this is a reality check," "this is just a creativity conversation," "this changed everything for me") — calmly, early, and without justification — before content lands? · *Mechanism:* the nervous system organizes behavior around the first category it receives; arguments that arrive after the frame are downstream and decorative. The flatter and more unjustified the label sounds, the stronger it holds — flowery framing invites rejection; boring framing reads as ambient fact. · *Strong:* declarative category in the first ~3s, no defense offered. *Mid:* category present but hedged or delayed past 5s. *Weak:* no framing — the viewer assigns their own category. *(src: instant-control-formula)* · *Extends §2.1 rapid-context — Kallaway wants the subject clear fast; this wants the* interpretive frame *set fast, a distinct lever.*
- **High-valence category trigger (permission/shield)** — *Signal:* is content placed inside a hard-wired category word — safety, care, expert guidance, professionalism, protecting the vulnerable — that pre-loads moral weight? · *Mechanism:* the category is a permission package: inside SAFETY, dissent feels reckless; inside CARE, criticism feels cruel; inside EXPERT, doubt feels ignorant. The frame defends itself — pushback reads as a character flaw, not a position, so the creator never has to answer objections. · *Strong:* category stated plainly and uncontested early, with soft moral escalation. *Mid:* category implied, not stated. *Weak:* no category architecture; claims are debatable on merits. *(src: instant-control-formula, cognitive-virology)*
- **Pre-approval over persuasion** — *Signal:* are authority / consensus / suppression / moral-virtue signals stacked *before* any claim is established ("experts agree," "everyone's starting to see this," "this keeps getting censored," "good people understand this")? · *Mechanism:* these shortcuts pre-approve the message rather than argue it — agreement is extracted before reasoning engages. The tell of success is that it feels *inevitable, not argued*; if the viewer feels actively persuaded, entry failed. Of these, moral framing is the most covert because it feels like ethics, not influence. · *Strong:* multiple shortcuts stacked in the first ~10s, claim arrives pre-approved. *Mid:* one shortcut present. *Weak:* argument-first, no shortcut scaffolding. *(src: cognitive-virology, dunkles-geheimnis, mind-weapon)*
- **Frame defang / reframe** — *Signal:* instead of rebutting a competing claim or criticism on the facts, does the creator name and label its *context* ("what's actually happening here is…," "this conversation always starts with one assumption")? · *Mechanism:* arguing inside a frame validates it; naming the frame makes it visible, and a visible frame loses its automatic hold — the viewer feels relief and intellectual permission to step out. · *Strong:* clean context-shift, the opposing frame is never engaged on its own terms. *Mid:* partial reframe mixed with fact-rebuttal. *Weak:* pure fact-vs-fact counter inside the given frame. *(src: instant-control-formula)*
- **Live micro-experiment (self-generated proof)** — *Signal:* does the creator issue a "try this right now" instruction (look at this and notice your reaction; name the feeling you're having) rather than only asserting? · *Mechanism:* real-time participation converts a passive viewer into a subject who generates their own evidence; a claim the viewer *felt* is unfalsifiable from inside their experience and lands as insight, not assertion. · *Strong:* brief, specific instruction that produces a noticeable internal reaction. *Mid:* experiment described but not enacted. *Weak:* claim stated with no demonstration. *(src: dunkles-geheimnis)*

### §2.6.3 — Limbic bypass & emotional encoding *(reach the decision engine, not the cortex)*
*Kallaway's emotional-beat coverage is thin (peak-end, identity/stance). This sub-group is the core add: emotion is not ornamentation, it is the encoding channel and an active suppressor of the rational filter. Decisions live in the limbic system; logic-first content addresses the wrong brain region.*

- **Limbic-first vs. placebo-logic** — *Signal:* does the video lead with felt stakes — story, visceral consequence, an emotion named — or with a logical stack (problem → solution → proof → CTA) and feature/stat lists? · *Mechanism:* the limbic system makes the decision; cortex-addressed content gets *evaluated* (compared, objected to, scrolled), not acted on. Pure-logic content is "placebo influence" — competently aimed at the brain region that doesn't decide. This diagnoses *why technically excellent educational content underperforms*. · *Strong:* emotional/visceral anchor carries the piece, logic in support. *Mid:* logic-dominant with a brief emotional opener. *Weak:* pure argument, no felt stakes. *(src: dangerous-influence, 2026-technik, psyop)*
- **Emotion as critical-thinking suppressor** — *Signal:* does the piece generate strong arousal (fear, outrage, awe, hope) early — and is the emotional charge carrying claims that have little verifiable backing? · *Mechanism:* high arousal *actively inhibits* prefrontal appraisal (not merely distracts) — the payload lands before skepticism activates. Outrage beats nuance, certainty beats humility, in the replication race. This is a double-edged read: it is a strong **share/retention** signal *and* a **credibility risk** flag for §5. · *Strong:* high arousal in the first ~3s, identity/moral stakes named. *Mid:* emotional framing present but secondary. *Weak:* information-first, emotion delayed or absent. *(src: psyop, cognitive-virology)*
- **Pre-existing emotion hijack (relief-seeking)** — *Signal:* does the open name or mirror a *specific, currently-felt, high-amplitude* emotion the target audience is living right now — not a generic pain point — and position the video as relief? · *Mechanism:* attaching to an emotion already at maximum amplitude is categorically stronger than manufacturing one; in relief-seeking mode the cognitive filter is functionally suspended and the idea enters as resolution rather than claim. · *Strong:* acute, live emotion named in precise language + resolution positioned as the payoff within ~5s. *Mid:* general pain point addressed. *Weak:* hypothetical/abstract emotional framing. *(src: cognitive-virology)*
- **Trauma-speed micro-story** — *Signal:* is there a compressed, high-intensity emotional micro-story (sudden loss, reversal, a wound) built to hit in under ~60s, with clear stakes and a sharp turn? · *Mechanism:* a single high-emotion event encodes behavior at the speed of trauma — far faster and more durably than a long arc. Counter-intuitively for short-form: *brevity + intensity > depth + length*; the emotion stamps the message as memory, not content. · *Strong:* visceral micro-story, clear stakes, sudden reversal. *Mid:* emotional anecdote without the compression/intensity. *Weak:* emotion described ("it was hard") rather than evoked. *(src: dangerous-influence)* · *Extends §2.2 head-fake — the turn here is emotional encoding speed, not a prediction-error reveal.*
- **Emotion-naming (felt-seen)** — *Signal:* does the creator name the viewer's likely internal state *precisely and before they'd find the word* — "that's not frustration, it's grief," "you told yourself it was fine," "you made yourself smaller"? Naming an *internal experience*, not shared external circumstances. · *Mechanism:* accurate mirroring of a private inner state registers as "another human saw what's happening inside me" — defenses drop, the message routes to cortex not amygdala, and the viewer shifts from watcher to personally-addressed. Must be accurate; an empty guess raises the wall harder. · *Strong:* specific, rarely-voiced, audience-accurate internal state named. *Mid:* general emotional validation. *Weak:* generic "I know it's hard." *(src: fbi-socrates, dangerous-influence, dunkles-geheimnis, scar-tissue)* · *Comment-section read: involuntary responses ("why am I crying," "had to pause") signal limbic engagement and predict higher velocity than "great points / so true" (cortex). (needs comments)*
- **Reach behind the mask** — *Signal:* does the content address the viewer's hidden self-doubt or private contradiction rather than their public-facing identity/goals? · *Mechanism:* conventional content validates the mask (the LinkedIn-resume self); content that names the private fear the viewer holds but hasn't voiced reaches the unguarded self — the viewer doesn't feel persuaded, they feel *seen*, which bonds them to the creator. A video that flatters the public identity is weaker than one that acknowledges the private contradiction. · *Strong:* names a specific private fear/shame the target recognizes as their own, without judgment. *Mid:* general emotional resonance, stays surface. *Weak:* addresses only aspirational/public identity. *(src: dangerous-influence, scar-tissue)*
- **Resonant truth (the body recognizes it)** — *Signal:* does the creator articulate a universal lived experience the audience has *had but never heard named cleanly* — the "they named the thing I couldn't name" response? · *Mechanism:* the limbic authenticity filter screens out polished fabrication and responds to resonant truth; the body holds it after the rational mind forgets the framing — both more powerful and more durable than technique. Pairs with congruent broadcast (§2.6.1): a true line delivered performatively still misses. · *Strong:* a specific, widely-lived, rarely-named truth. *Mid:* emotionally resonant but not a novel articulation. *Weak:* inspirational generality, no grounding in lived experience. *(src: dangerous-influence)*
- **Dual-pole contrast (dream × nightmare).** *Signal:* does the piece pair a vivid desired future with a vivid 'if nothing changes' cost, in close narrative proximity? · *Mechanism:* the brain doesn't crave change until the status quo feels unbearable; the nightmare pole is limbic fuel, not optional stakes — aspiration alone is neurologically insufficient. · *Strong:* both poles vivid, specific, personalized, adjacent. *Mid:* one pole strong, other generic. *Weak:* aspiration-only, no cost framing. *(src: 2026-technik)* · Distinct from §2.6.4 threat-over-value (that's attention capture at the hook; this is motivational craving across the arc).

### §2.6.4 — Attention capture & focus mechanics *(the gate that must open first)*
*Kallaway's scroll-stop (§2.1) covers visual differentiation. This sub-group adds the **mechanism** under it — novelty as an involuntary focus switch that raises suggestibility, threat-priority over value, and the pacing constraints that exploit it. Focus is the gate: authority, emotion, and logic cannot land until it opens.*

- **Novelty as suggestibility switch** — *Signal:* does the hook violate the viewer's prediction — counter-intuitive claim, unexpected visual/format, sensory pattern-break — in the opening frame (not a louder version of the expected opener)? · *Mechanism:* novelty fires an involuntary brain-stem focus switch that doesn't just grab attention, it *raises suggestibility* — critical gatekeeping is briefly downregulated, so the next message lands with abnormal force. Distinct from "be interesting": this is a pre-conscious gate. · *Strong:* genuine pattern-break in the first ~1.5s, unpredictable from the thumbnail. *Mid:* fresh angle but novelty arrives after ~5s. *Weak:* genre-standard opener, no interrupt. *(src: compliance, 2026-technik, scary-loophole, teil-2, influence)* · *Extends §2.1 scroll-stop / §2.0 prediction loop — net-new angle is the suggestibility window, not just the thumb-stop.*
- **Message-in-the-window** — *Signal:* does the core claim land *immediately* after the novelty/shock trigger (within ~1–2s), or is the open novel but the point delayed 5–10s? · *Mechanism:* the elevated-suggestibility window is short; a message delivered into it bypasses normal gatekeeping, a message that arrives after it has closed lands on a re-armed critical filter. · *Strong:* core claim within ~2s of the trigger. *Mid:* novel open but point delayed past the window. *Weak:* no trigger before the main message. *(src: compliance)*
- **Threat-over-value framing** — *Signal:* does the hook encode a loss/risk/consequence ("this mistake will cost you," "most people never figure this out") rather than only a gain? · *Mechanism:* the survival brain dedicates more, non-optional resources to threat-detection than to opportunity; a credible threat-signal captures focus more reliably than a pure value-signal. · *Strong:* explicit consequence framing. *Mid:* implicit risk ("most people never…"). *Weak:* pure gain hook, no loss dimension. *(src: teil-2, influence)* · *Extends §2.1 curiosity gap — the gap here is specifically a threat asymmetry, a calibration not a new gap type. Note overlap with §3 "generic fear kicker": reward a* specific, concept-tied *threat; an attachable-to-anything "if you don't do this you'll fail" still fails per §3.*
- **Pattern-interrupt placement** — *Signal:* are deliberate environmental/format disruptions (unexpected cut, scene/framing change, sensory mismatch) placed at attention-decay points (~5s, ~15s, ~30s), not just a uniform-paced clip? · *Mechanism:* the brain runs old consumption scripts on familiar, predictable stimuli and drops attention; a pattern-interrupt terminates the passive script, forces conscious focus, and re-encodes. · *Strong:* multiple interrupts at decay points. *Mid:* one strong pattern-break. *Weak:* uniform environment, predictable pacing throughout. *(src: 2026-technik, scary-loophole)* · *Extends §2.2 re-hook — Kallaway's re-hook reopens a* prediction loop; *this reopens the* focus gate *via environmental novelty. Adjacent, often co-fire.*
- **Pre-literate hook test** *(diagnostic heuristic)* — *Signal:* would the hook capture a pre-literate brain — on pure sensory novelty, threat, or high-value signal — before any text/argument is decoded? · *Mechanism:* the attention gate must open before authority/emotion/logic can reach the decision layer; a hook that only works once you read a paragraph is operating at the weakest (electrical) layer. Useful as a bias-check on hooks the analyst or creator is attached to. · *Strong:* fires on sensory novelty/threat even muted and text-free. *Mid:* needs one short alarming phrase. *Weak:* only works if the viewer reads/follows an argument. *(src: teil-2)* · *Reinforces §2.1 mute-readability from the survival-brain angle.*

### §2.6.5 — Identity & shareability *(the self-concept lever)*
*Kallaway's identity/stance (§2.3) covers shares driven by stated opinion. This sub-group is deeper: content that **installs or recognizes an identity** in the viewer, and sharing as identity-performance rather than information transfer. Identity beats logistics.*

- **Identity recognition / installation** — *Signal:* does the creator attribute a specific trait to the viewer as an *observation*, not a request — "you're the kind of person who…," "people like you already know this" — framed as noticing, not flattering? · *Mechanism:* an accepted identity triggers neurological self-enforcement; once the viewer adopts the label (even by not correcting it) their own consistency drive makes them act in line with it. Recognition framing ("pointing at what's already there") meets near-zero resistance vs. assignment. · *Strong:* specific trait named as recognition, tied to the desired action, space left to accept. *Mid:* general "you seem like someone who cares about X." *Weak:* "our audience values X" / no labeling. *(src: fbi-socrates, dunkles-geheimnis, mind-weapon)* · *Extends §2.3 personalization — adds the self-enforcement mechanism that personalization alone doesn't name.*
- **Identity upgrade, not negation** — *Signal:* when the creator reshapes how the viewer sees themselves, do they offer a *more accurate, more dignified* frame ("you're not failing, your system is under load," "that's not weakness, that's recalibration") rather than contradicting ("you're wrong about yourself")? · *Mechanism:* negating an identity triggers instant defense — the viewer evaluates the speaker instead of the reframe. An upgrade that names the *function* of a behavior (not a trait) and requires abandoning nothing is accepted as more dignified. Reframes that demand abandonment trigger entrenchment. · *Strong:* function-named upgrade, dignity preserved, nothing to give up. *Mid:* reframe present but uses "not X" framing. *Weak:* direct identity challenge / correction. *(src: instant-control-formula, mind-weapon)*
- **Identity-frame over logistics** — *Signal:* does the creator pivot from the object-level (tactics, money, tools, features) to an identity/legacy frame — "this isn't about X, it's about whether you're the kind of person who…"? · *Mechanism:* once self-concept stakes are in play, logical/object-level content is overridden; the decision becomes about who the viewer wants to be. Identity beats logistics. · *Strong:* explicit frame-shift to identity, no return to the object-level. *Mid:* identity stakes alongside logistics. *Weak:* stays object-level, or a generic "imagine your future self." *(src: fbi-socrates, instant-control-formula)*
- **In-group / conspiracy seal** — *Signal:* does the creator construct an exclusive in-group with the viewer inside — "most people aren't ready to hear this," "only X% of people get this," "if you're watching this you're different"? · *Mechanism:* tribal belonging is a primary driver; selecting the viewer into a circle creates loyalty, and sharing the content becomes *identity performance* — opposing camps run identical identity-upgrade loops, the content is incidental. · *Strong:* explicit in-group/out-group with the viewer inside + specificity. *Mid:* implied tribe membership. *Weak:* generic "for the ambitious ones" / no identity hook. *(src: fbi-socrates, cognitive-virology, scar-tissue, instant-control-formula)* · *Extends §2.3 identity/stance — Kallaway: take a side to provoke shares; here: confer membership so the* share is self-expression.*
- **Tribe-norm framing** — *Signal:* does the creator invoke a tight shared identity ("if you're a creator…," "our community…") and state the content as *what that group does/believes*? · *Mechanism:* tribal identity framing activates a conformity governor — viewers filter through "is this what my tribe does?" before rational evaluation; group consensus (visible comments, "everyone agrees," counts) can trigger compliance the way authority does. · *Strong:* tight, self-identifiable label + norm stated as group behavior. *Mid:* broad audience label only. *Weak:* no tribal framing. *(src: 2026-technik, influence, compliance, teil-2)*
- **Borrowed-research identity install** — *Signal:* does the creator cite "research / a study / experts found" that conveniently validates the viewer's aspirational identity or the video's premise, with a tidy trait list? · *Mechanism:* an external pseudo-objective source bypasses resistance to being *told* what you are — the viewer absorbs the framing as neutral information rather than evaluating it. · *Strong:* specific-sounding study with a clear trait list mapped to the desired identity. *Mid:* vague "experts say." *Weak:* no external reference. *(src: mind-weapon)* · *Credibility caution for §5/§4: unverifiable "a study found" is a luck/inflation-adjacent signal, not earned proof — reward its retention effect, flag the evidence gap.*

### §2.6.6 — Compliance & conversion mechanics *(the close, and the structure that gets there)*
*Kallaway's CTA layer (§2.4) is desire-outcome and low-friction next step. This sub-group adds the behavioral close mechanics: micro-commitment laddering, calm non-pressure, silence, scarcity-as-reflex, and the future-self collapse — plus the full installed sequence.*

- **Micro-commitment ladder** — *Signal:* does the piece chain small accepted asks (agree with an obvious statement, like, comment) before a larger behavioral/belief ask, each step contingent on the last? · *Mechanism:* each accepted micro-yes shifts self-concept slightly and raises the consistency cost of refusing the next; by the real ask, compliance feels like identity-consistency, not influence. · *Strong:* explicit escalating ladder, each step linked to the prior. *Mid:* two-step (small → large) with linking language. *Weak:* single large CTA, no prior commitment. *(src: compliance, psyop)*
- **Calm non-pressure close** — *Signal:* is the delivery calm and non-demanding even at the ask / on high-stakes content — measured persistence rather than rising urgency? · *Mechanism:* absence of overt pressure removes the viewer's justification to resist ("nothing threatening is happening, so no reason to stop"), stripping the socially-acceptable exit ramp and keeping them open. · *Strong:* consistently calm through the ask and any provocation. *Mid:* mostly calm, occasional urgency spikes. *Weak:* demanding / high-pressure delivery. *(src: compliance)* · *Reliability caveat: low inter-rater reliability; calm vs. flat is hard to separate from transcript±visual.*
- **Silence after the turn** *(needs video)* — *Signal:* is there a deliberate held pause (≈1–3s, unbroken by music or cut) after a key reframe or high-stakes line? · *Mechanism:* silence lets the viewer's brain complete the thought and arrive at the conclusion itself; self-generated conclusions are the most durable form of compliance, and filling the silence robs the viewer of that. · *Strong:* deliberate held pause post-reframe. *Mid:* short pause, quickly filled. *Weak:* continuous talking, no space. *(src: instant-control-formula)*
- **Scarcity-as-reflex (positioned after desire)** — *Signal:* is scarcity (countdown, limited slots, closing window) placed *after* desire is established, not before? · *Mechanism:* scarcity at peak desire fires an ancestral resource-grab reflex that bypasses cost-benefit; the timer is a stimulus for a pre-cognitive act, not a logical argument. Front-loaded scarcity with no primed desire mostly misfires. · *Strong:* scarcity after a primed desire + social-proof stack present. *Mid:* scarcity present but not positioned after the desire peak. *Weak:* generic "limited time" with no desire priming. *(src: scary-loophole)* · *Note §3: power-words (free/secret/cheap/banned) and on-platform "everyone is doing this NOW" urgency can throttle reach — flag, don't assume fatal.*
- **Capability pre-commitment** — *Signal:* before the CTA, does the video show the *simplest possible* demonstration by someone clearly less capable than the target ("a four-year-old did it," "even my grandma")? · *Mechanism:* downward capability comparison removes the "I can't do that" objection at a pre-verbal level; the behavior is pre-committed before the ask, so the CTA triggers an already-made decision. · *Strong:* lowest-bar demonstrator + explicit "anyone can do this" bridge + immediate CTA. *Mid:* demonstration shown, no accessibility bridge. *Weak:* CTA with no pre-commitment setup. *(src: scary-loophole)* · *Extends §2.4 actionability — Kallaway: short distance to implement; this: prove it's easy by who does it.*
- **Future-self collapse (prospective memory)** — *Signal:* does the creator use future-perfect framing with sensory specificity — "a year from now when you look back…," "the morning after you post this, the first feeling is…" — treating the action as already taken? · *Mechanism:* concrete sensory detail makes the brain process a hypothetical as a real memory; the deliberation phase collapses because the decision feels already made, and *not* acting feels like giving up a feeling already experienced. Collapses the temporal-empathy gap (the future self becomes perceptible, so the brain bonds to it). · *Strong:* future-perfect tense + specific sensory scene + no hedging. *Mid:* "imagine when you've done this." *Weak:* generic "picture yourself succeeding." *(src: fbi-socrates, 2026-technik)* · *Extends §2.4 desire-based hook — adds the temporal mechanism (treat-as-done) the desire-hook templates don't specify.*
- **Installed sequence (full stack)** — *Signal:* does the video run the layered arc — altitude/frame set → category lock → identity reframe → emotional/desire beat → deliberate closing silence — as a sequenced whole rather than isolated tactics? · *Mechanism:* the layers compound (frame sets momentum, category freezes permission, identity organizes self-concept, silence completes self-persuasion); a full clean run makes the final state feel like the viewer's own conclusion. The cleanest version is invisible — "you could videotape it and not see what's happening." A self-referential reveal ("I've been doing this to you the whole time") converts the structure into proof-of-concept and an awe/trust spike. · *Strong:* all stages present, deliberate pacing. *Mid:* 3–4 stages, sequencing partly collapsed. *Weak:* 1–2 stages, no sequencing. *(src: instant-control-formula, cognitive-virology, fbi-socrates, 2026-technik)* · *This is the behavioral-layer analog of §2.2's loop integrity — grade structural completeness, not any single beat.*

### §2.6.7 — Cognitive-loop & open-tension levers *(content that runs after it ends)*
*Kallaway's loops (§2.2) are within-video prediction loops that must close. This sub-group adds tension levers aimed at **post-view** processing and self-persuasion — distinct enough to keep separate, adjacent enough to cross-reference.*

- **Dissonance + relief path** — *Signal:* does the video name a contradiction the viewer likely holds ("you say you want X but keep doing Y") *and* supply the resolution path? · *Mechanism:* dissonance is a compulsive itch; the brain resolves it by the path of least resistance. Name the gap *and* hand the relief and the viewer adopts it as self-motivated change (durable); name the gap with no relief and they double down (backfire). Direct factual correction of an identity-anchored belief *strengthens* it — so contradiction without a relief path is a failure mode, not a lever. · *Strong:* tension named + viewer sees themselves in the gap + relief path offered. *Mid:* tension named, no relief. *Weak:* generic "did you know," no personal relevance. *(src: mind-weapon, psyop)*
- **Self-discovery framing** — *Signal:* does the creator invite reflection / frame the insight as something the viewer *already sensed* ("you've probably already noticed…") rather than lecturing ("you should do X")? · *Mechanism:* a conclusion that feels self-arrived meets low reactance and sticks; an imposed one is resisted and reversed. Self-motivated change is durable, sold change is not. · *Strong:* explicit invitation to self-discover. *Mid:* neutral information delivery. *Weak:* direct prescription. *(src: mind-weapon)*
- **Unfinished-business open loop** — *Signal:* does the piece end on an open pattern — a half-stated implication, a future trigger ("next time you see X, you'll notice…") — rather than a fully closed conclusion? · *Mechanism:* an unresolved pattern leaves cognitive business running after the video ends (Zeigarnik-like), driving involuntary replay and "I'll watch their next one" return behavior. Making the viewer feel clever (they complete the thought) generates ownership. · *Strong:* explicit open loop with a future trigger planted. *Mid:* implied open question. *Weak:* fully closed, no mental residue. *(src: cognitive-virology)* · *Tension with §2.2 validation — Kallaway wants the in-video loop* closed *with a payoff; this deliberately leaves a* meta-loop *open for after. Both can be true: close the episode's prediction, leave a forward hook. Don't penalize a clean payoff for also planting a next-video loop.*
- **Binary compression** — *Signal:* does the video compress a nuanced topic into a two-camp frame (awake/asleep, winners/losers, smart/naive) and sort the viewer onto the winning side? · *Mechanism:* a binary eliminates the cognitive friction skepticism needs and activates identity-sorting, which drives engagement and shares — the simpler the binary, the faster the spread. · *Strong:* explicit binary with identity stakes named. *Mid:* implicit us-vs-them, no explicit binary. *Weak:* nuanced, multiple perspectives. *(src: cognitive-virology)* · *Credibility caution: high binary compression is a strong* share *signal and a* substance/honesty *risk — pair with §2.5 non-obvious-take and §5 (don't read engineered division as earned insight).*
- **Striking reframe metaphor** — *Signal:* does the creator reframe a familiar concept with one anti-intuitive, mechanically-grounded metaphor ("language is a parasite," "beliefs are outdated software")? · *Mechanism:* a counter-intuitive reframe fires curiosity and mild dissonance at once, compelling continued watching, and gives the idea a portable handle that travels in shares. · *Strong:* biologically/mechanically grounded and genuinely counter-intuitive. *Mid:* vivid but familiar. *Weak:* vague or decorative. *(src: dunkles-geheimnis, scar-tissue)* · *Extends §2.5 non-obvious take — adds the* metaphor-as-handle *delivery angle.*
- **Expectancy (predict-and-payoff).** *Signal:* does the video signpost its structure ('first… then… by the end') and promise a payoff it visibly delivers? · *Mechanism:* predictability lowers the viewer's threat-detection load so they stay for the reward instead of scanning for the exit; high expectancy raises trust and makes every other lever easier to land. · *Strong:* explicit promise + matched structure + delivered payoff. *Mid:* implicit structure, no payoff cue. *Weak:* random jump-cuts, no through-line. *(src: human-influence-map)* · Distinct from §2.6.7 open-loop (that leaves a *forward* loop open; this *closes* the episode's promised loop).
