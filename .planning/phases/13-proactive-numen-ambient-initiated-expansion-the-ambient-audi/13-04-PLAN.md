<objective>
Mount the persistent presence in the composer and give it a live spotlight: dock `<AmbientPresence>` as a sticky strip at the top of the `composer-thread-region` scroll div, and drive its `focus` prop with scroll-spy (IntersectionObserver over the card elements, ambient default) + explicit tap (wins momentarily, RESEARCH Pitfall 4) + type-to-room (sets subject to the typed thought), defaulting to the latest card on load (D-02). The presence reads the active audience from the composer's already-lifted `selectedAudience` state and `reducedMotion` from the already-wired `usePrefersReducedMotion` — no new fetch (RESEARCH A5).

Tap-to-focus seam (the load-bearing detail): the shipped `LensTrigger` hardcodes its `onClick` to `setOpen(true)` and exposes NO tap/focus passthrough prop (verified: LensTrigger.tsx L57). Forking it to add one would violate D-05/D-06 ("extend, do NOT duplicate"). The non-forking seam is a capture-phase handler on the `data-ambient-card` wrapper this plan already adds around each card: `onClickCapture={() => focusByTap(id)}` fires in the capture phase, BEFORE the inner LensTrigger's bubble-phase `onClick` — so a card tap runs focus AND opens the Lens (not mutually exclusive), with zero change to the shipped LensTrigger. CardReactionAtRest (Plan 13-02) keeps its tap as Lens-open-only; it gains no new prop.

Purpose: This is the wiring that makes the audience "always felt" and the spotlight a moving view onto each card's durable reaction — never a running average, zero new model calls on re-focus. It closes the phase: the three doors (per-card tap from Plan 02, presence spotlight + type-to-room from Plan 03) all converge on the one Lens.
Output: A focus-state hook + the composer mount + a happy-dom test for the focus resolution, then phase verification.
</objective>
