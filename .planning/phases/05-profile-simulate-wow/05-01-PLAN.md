<objective>
Define the two new typed-block contracts (`profile-read`, `reaction-distribution`) and wire the entire block-rendering + chain-handoff rails so the downstream runners (05-04, 05-05) and routes have a stable, registered contract to emit against — no scavenger hunt.

Purpose: This is the interface-first foundation for the phase. Pitfall 4 is the headline risk: a block must be registered in `BLOCK_REGISTRY` + `BlockUnionSchema` + `BLOCK_COMPONENTS` together, or it silently degrades to the `__unsupported__` placeholder on reload. PROF-04's chain CTA is one registry entry (D-07 reuse the rails) — and the load-bearing seam of the whole SIMU-03 wow is one hop: the profile-read card's `savedAudienceId` must reach the Simulate POST body as `audienceId`, so this plan test-enforces that mapping with a pure helper (Warning-1) rather than leaving it to the manual UAT. Bands-only honesty (D-06) is enforced structurally by `.strict()`. The two new schemas are co-located in a sibling `profile-blocks.ts` so `blocks.ts` stays under the project's 500-line limit (it is at 468 lines and the two rich schemas would push it over — additive, no behavior change).
Output: Two new block schemas + types (in `profile-blocks.ts`), registry + union + component-map entries, two plain renderer components, the `buildSimulateRequest` seam helper + its unit test, the `profile→simulate` chain-handoff entry, and round-trip persistence proof.
</objective>
