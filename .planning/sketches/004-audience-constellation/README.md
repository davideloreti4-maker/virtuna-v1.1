# Sketch 004: Constellation + Persistent Reaction Feed

## Two problems addressed
1. **Replay was unreadable** — a caption flashing each reaction for ~1s. You can't *follow* a focus group that way.
2. **Node-cloud question** — should we adopt Artificial Societies' connected-dot society?

## Position on the node-cloud: steal the soul, not the swarm
Artificial Societies' magic is **density (N=100s)** + emergence + anonymity. Numen's
unit is **10 named, calibrated personas** = the hero object, a relationship.
- 10 nodes in a force-graph = sparse + sad; loses the faces/names/verbatims that ARE the differentiation.
- Edges imply social propagation we don't model = decoration / the fake-citation trap.
- A 150-node graph is a desktop artifact on a mobile-first rebrand.

**Synthesis built here:**
- **Constellation of 10**, clustered by disposition (Receptive / Critical / Analytical), **named** (initials).
- **Edges = affinity/similarity** (honest — engine has persona attributes), NOT fake social ties.
- On **Replay**, reaction **ripples** node→node along affinity edges AND writes to a **persistent feed**.
- Dense swarm **reserved for population-scale tests** (P8 multi-audience / P10) where density is real.

## Replay fix
- Persistent **reaction feed**: each reaction lands as a row (avatar, name, reaction, timestamp, quote) and **stays**. Scroll back, tap to expand the "why", "Ask directly".
- Constellation = the stage (ripple); feed = the readable record. They play together.
