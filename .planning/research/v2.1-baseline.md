## apidojo Asymmetries

The corpus was scraped via `clockworks/tiktok-scraper` (FREE-tier escape hatch; see Methodology below). Although the codebase has migrated to `apidojo/tiktok-scraper` for future scrapes, the actual `full.2026-05-11` corpus was collected via clockworks. As a result:

- `follower_count` is **populated for 100% of rows** (clockworks asset)
- `sound_name` is **populated for 100% of rows** (clockworks asset)

The eval-runner uses `follower_tier` (derived from `follower_count`) as a downstream feature. The v2.1 `trends.ts` matches `sound_name` against trending sounds for trend enrichment. Both signals are live in this baseline.

**Forward-looking note:** A future apidojo-scraped corpus would have `follower_count = null` and `sound_name = null`. This baseline would NOT be directly comparable to such a corpus. Future scrape planning should account for this asymmetry when deciding whether to create a new `corpus_version`.

## Interpretation

v2.1 is barely better than guessing on this corpus. Macro-F1 of 0.294 with 3 buckets means the engine fails most of its predictions. Chance level on a balanced 3-class problem is 0.333 — v2.1 achieves 0.294, meaning it is BELOW random on the actual (imbalanced) corpus.

The 10.6% viral recall is the headline failure: the engine catches only 1 of every ~10 viral videos. ECE of 0.372 indicates the score → bucket mapping (`bucketFromScore`) is poorly calibrated against empirical view-distribution buckets.

The Spearman rank correlation within each niche is 0.0 across all 5 niches — the engine's continuous score has zero monotone relationship with actual view counts within any niche. The score is decorrelated from the ground truth.

**The failure pattern in `failure_cases` is revealing:** All 10 recorded failures are severity=2 (under predicted as viral). The engine assigns scores of 74-79 to videos that are genuinely underperforming. This suggests the rule-scoring stage inflates scores for text-heavy captions or certain hashtag patterns that appear in educational/comedy/beauty content regardless of actual outcome.

This number is the hard, measurable baseline that engine v3 must beat. Per D-18 (sliding-scale improvement rule), with baseline macro_f1 = 0.294 (≤ 0.40):

- **v3 must achieve: macro_f1 ≥ 0.338** (0.294 × 1.15 = 15% relative improvement required)

## Threshold Rule (D-18)

The required improvement is computed by `requiredImprovementFor(baselineMacroF1)` in `src/lib/engine/corpus/eval-config.ts`:

```
baseline ≤ 0.40  →  15% relative improvement required
0.40 < baseline ≤ 0.55  →  10% relative improvement required
baseline > 0.55  →  7% relative improvement required
```

With baseline macro_f1 = 0.294:
- Required threshold = 0.294 × 1.15 = **0.3381**
- Per-niche regression gate (D-15): no niche may regress more than 5 pp vs this baseline
- Statistical gate (D-17): improvement must be significant at α=0.05 via paired bootstrap (200 iterations minimum)

Phase 4+ XGBoost + behavioral-science features target: **macro_f1 > 0.338**, ECE < 0.37, viral_recall > 0.11.

## Threshold Formula (D-09)

Each niche's bucket boundaries were empirically derived from the filtered scrape distribution:
- **viralFloor** = P80 of `views` for that niche on the 180-day filtered cache
- **underCeiling** = P40 of `views` for that niche on the 180-day filtered cache

Sealed values (D-13 immutable per `corpus_version = full.2026-05-11`):

| Niche | viralFloor | underCeiling | Separation (ratio) |
|---|---|---|---|
| beauty | 5,120,000 | 250,840 | 20× |
| fitness | 5,480,000 | 1,164,240 | 5× |
| edu | 2,000,000 | 368,500 | 5× |
| comedy | 25,900,000 | 9,000,000 | 2.88× |
| lifestyle | 871,200 | 153,540 | 6× |

Comedy separation (2.88×) is just below the 3× sanity floor — accepted as genuine distribution compression in trending comedy hashtags. At P30, the comedy view count was 6.88M, meaning a third of all trending comedy videos already have 6.88M views. This is not a calibration error; it reflects the reality that comedy goes viral at extremely high thresholds. Documented inline in `thresholds.ts`.

## Known Limitations

### 1. Niche purity pollution

`#fyp` is the #1 hashtag in beauty/comedy/lifestyle and #2 in edu/fitness. The `TRENDING_FEED_HASHTAGS=[niche, "fyp"]` design pulls generic FYP content alongside niche-specific content. This reduces creator diversity (9-10 distinct creators per viral bucket vs D-01 target ≥34) and surfaces non-niche viral content.

**Mitigation for Phase 4+ corpus refresh:** Multi-source scraping — search URLs (which support apidojo's dateRange filter), curated profile lists, music URLs — alongside cleaner niche-specific hashtags. Tracked in `feedback_corpus_scrape_diversity.md` (memory).

### 2. Recency ceiling — fixed in code 2026-05-11

`clockworks/tiktok-scraper` silently ignored `oldestPostDate`. Discovered during calibration: 405/568 quality-filtered rows were >90 days old (median 9 months, P75 3.2 years). Many "viral" rows were years-old non-niche content skewing calibration.

**Permanent fix:** `MAX_AGE_MS = 180 days` client-side filter added to `src/lib/engine/corpus/normalize-scrape.ts` (commit 7540a96). Applies to both clockworks and apidojo paths. Future scrapes apply this filter automatically.

### 3. Creator diversity below D-01 target

Viral buckets have 9-10 distinct creators each (D-01 target: ≥34). Acceptable for a single-engine baseline (apples-to-apples across engine versions on the same corpus). Increasing diversity requires higher `maxItems` and broader hashtag coverage — addressed in Phase 4+ corpus refresh.

### 4. Comedy bucket compression (2.88× separation)

viralFloor (25.9M) / underCeiling (9.0M) = 2.88× — just below the 3× sanity floor. Accepted as genuine distribution compression in trending comedy hashtags. Documented in `thresholds.ts` comment block.

### 5. One row failed during eval

`rows_failed = 1` in the benchmark_results row. The failing row (unknown corpus_row_id — not recorded in the JSON) crashed the eval pipeline and was excluded from all metrics. The eval-runner is fault-tolerant; this failure does not invalidate the baseline.

### 6. Zero Spearman correlation within niches

`spearman_within_niche` is 0.0 for all 5 niches. The engine's continuous score has zero monotone relationship with actual view counts within any single niche. This confirms the score is not a reliable ranking signal, not just a poor classifier.

### 7. audio_analysis is a no-op

Stage latency for `audio_analysis` is 0ms for all percentiles — the audio stage returns immediately. Audio signal weight in the v2.1 aggregator is therefore zero in practice. Engine v3 (Phase 6) will implement real audio analysis.
