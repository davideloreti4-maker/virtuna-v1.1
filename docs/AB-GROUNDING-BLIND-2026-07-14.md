# Grounded vs ungrounded hooks — BLIND READ

**The question (handoff §3.1): does grounding actually make a BETTER hook?** Eight asks, eight niches, eight matching creator profiles. Same ask, same profile, same platform, same model in both arms — **grounding is the only variable.**

## How to read this

Each case shows **Set A** and **Set B**. One is grounded, one is not, and **which is which changes from case to case.** The key is at the bottom, below a spoiler gap. Read all eight, mark your preference per case, *then* scroll.

**Only the hook lines are shown.** Three other fields were withheld, on purpose:

- **the source receipt** — only a grounded hook can cite one, so printing it would announce the arm;
- **`mechanism`** — the grounded arm leads with craft slugs ("Open Loop:", "Pattern Interrupt:") while the ungrounded arm writes prose (handoff §3.4), which announces the arm just as loudly;
- **the band score** — it comes from our own Flash SIM. Using our own simulator to score our own grounding is circular, and showing it here would anchor your read to exactly the number the handoff warns is not evidence.

All three are captured in the **appendix below the key** — nothing is hidden, it is only sequenced so it cannot contaminate the judgement. **The hook line is the deliverable; judge that.**

## Case 1 — founder personal branding

> **Ask:** _why founders should post daily even when it feels cringe_
> **Creator:** early-stage B2B SaaS founders, technical, allergic to marketing · goal: inbound leads for a fractional CMO offer

**Set A**

1. I forced myself to post for 30 days straight. Here is the one metric that actually moved.
2. If it feels easy, you are invisible.
3. Stop waiting for the 'right' brand voice. It does not exist yet.
4. Posting daily feels cringe. That is the point.
5. You are not posting too much. You are posting too safely.

**Set B**

1. Your competitors aren't smarter. They're just less embarrassed.
2. Open on a screen recording of a blank draft folder with 40+ unsaved drafts. Cut to face: 'This is why you're invisible.'
3. Posting daily feels cringe because you are still the main character.
4. I posted for 90 days straight. The first 60 were painful. The last 30 paid for the year.
5. Stop trying to be interesting. Start being consistent.

## Case 2 — strength training

> **Ask:** _why lifting heavy 3x a week beats spending an hour on the treadmill_
> **Creator:** men and women 30-45 who joined a gym and feel lost in the free-weight area · goal: sign-ups for an 8-week beginner barbell program

**Set A**

1. Your hour on the treadmill isn’t building muscle—it’s just burning the fuel you need to lift heavy tomorrow.

3. Open on a close-up of a worn-out treadmill belt, then hard cut to a loaded barbell on the floor. No intro music.
4. Stop treating the treadmill like penance for eating bread.
5. I spent six months chasing a runner’s high and got joint pain. Here’s the switch that actually changed my body composition.

**Set B**

1. Here’s the #1 tip I’d use if I had to get strong but only had three hours a week.
2. Sweating isn’t progress. Are you making these beginner strength mistakes?
3. Did you know if you swap one hour of steady-state cardio for three heavy lifts, you’ll burn more fat at rest?
4. Stop treating the treadmill like cardio. It’s just slow, boring strength training with zero return.
5. Please don’t spend an hour on the treadmill! I am begging you to stop wasting your recovery.
