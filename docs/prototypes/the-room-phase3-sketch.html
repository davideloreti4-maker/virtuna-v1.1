why Three intents, not thirteen tools

The verbs split by your relationship to the artifact — which is the real difference between the old skills:

Make
create net-new — you have nothing yet
Test
judge something real — a video, your account
Ask
converse / probe — just a question

flow One field, three modes

Default is Make — type a topic, hit ↑, hooks generate and the room reacts. That's the 90% path; the menu never opens. The chip is a mode-switch on the same input: pick Test and it swaps to the upload affordance; pick Ask and the placeholder becomes "ask the room…". Two levels, not lossy — Make expands to its flavors.

kept Explore + Account stay ✓

Per your call — Explore sits under Make (discovery feeds what you make); Account Read sits under Test (it judges something real — your own posts). Both keep a home in the menu.

context The menu is audience-aware

changes The audience panel → the Room

Today a video Test renders its audience through the old AudienceLens; the Make skills use the new v6 Room. Phase 3 makes the Test speak the Room's language — named voices you can ask →, the population, the weak-spot, the rewrite. One audience surface, whether you Made or Tested.

stays The video-only Read is already good

You already reskinned these for flat-warm — they stay: the Hook decomposition, the Retention scrubber (the frame-by-frame watch-journey + the drop moment), the Score distribution. The Room replaces the personas panel, not the whole Read.

new The video earns a timeline Replay

A real video carries a per-second attention trace that text can't. So the video-Test Room adds one thing the text Room can't have: ▶ Replay in timeline — the voices react as the video plays, lighting up at the exact moment each viewer leans in or bails. The "full Read" the flagship skill deserves.

one line

Collapse the doing into Make / Test / Ask, and unify the seeing into one Room — so Test stops being a separate analytics world and becomes "the Room, on a real video, with a retention curve."
