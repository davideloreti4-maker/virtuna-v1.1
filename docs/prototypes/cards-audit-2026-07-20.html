Language A — the editorial verdict card

The bar

Hooks is the reference. The anatomy reads top-to-bottom as an argument: who it's for → the deliverable → the evidence it borrows → why it works → how the room reacted → one place for detail → one next step. This is a genuinely good card language, and five other surfaces already speak it.

Hooks · desktop · the reference anatomy

Why it works

  • The hero is the deliverable — the hook itself, first and biggest.
  • Proof is one unit: band dot+word, fraction, neutral bar, one verbatim, the room door.
  • Provenance demoted to the disclosure line (· SIM-1 Flash) — a footnote, not a headline.
  • One cream primary; Save is an icon, not a row.
Keep as the vocabulary source. Whatever we build next — grammar, generative layer — its primitives should be extracted from this card, not invented fresh.
Script (beat rows) · chat citations — same voice, different content shapes

The language already stretches

  • Script proves the spine can host a 5-beat body without breaking voice.
  • Citations prove the receipt row works standalone, N-up, fed by tool rows.
  • The run capsule (label → plan spine → ✓ receipt) gives every wait the same grammar.
Evidence the grammar hypothesis is sound: these are already compositions of ~8 recurring pieces — they're just hand-soldered per card today.

Language B — the dashboard tile

Different product

Explore doesn't share a single element with the hooks card. It speaks an older stat-dashboard dialect: mono-caps stat pairs (12× VS OWN), fit bars filled edge-to-edge in band color, a 4-icon metric quad per tile, and the cream primary — supposedly the one-per-card focal point — three times, each labeled with two fused verbs ("Remix → Read").

Explore · desktop — three tiles, three primaries, three filled bars

Concept problems, not drift

  • A tile is a mini-dashboard; the thread is editorial. Two products on one screen.
  • Band color as a filled bar is the exact pattern the contract bans — color is a mark, not a fill.
  • 4-icon stat quad per tile = 12 icons per card answering questions nobody asked yet.
  • "Remix → Read" fuses two actions into one button label.
  • Honesty is right (measured-only, no fabricated SIM proof) — keep that, lose the dialect.
Not fixable by polish. This card needs re-expression in language A: outlier = a receipt row (the citation card already renders scraped videos in-voice), fit = dot + word, one primary.
Account Read (half-migrated) · Explore at 390px — ~5 screens for 3 results

Account Read: half-in, half-out

  • Opens on the creator's name — no hero sentence; the payoff (the read) never headlines.
  • WHAT'S WORKING / WHAT TO FIX put data color on labels; the system's rule is color on marks, labels stay muted.
  • Mobile Explore is the density smoking gun: 1,849px tall for three results.
Account is one hero + two label fixes from language A. Explore is a rebuild.

Language C — the transcript

Says everything twice

The Text Read is the busiest surface in the thread. It states each audience's band three times (summary pill, section header, colored "Strong Read." lead-in), nests THE ROOM as a box-within-the-card, and captions all eight verbatims with a mono-caps attribution ladder (BOOTSTRAPPED FOUNDERS + GENERAL · THE NEWCOMER) louder than the quotes themselves.

Text Read · desktop — band ×3 per audience, room boxed, caps ladder ×8

Concept problems

  • It's a transcript, not a verdict. The reader does the synthesis the card should have done.
  • The group/row law inverted: every quote row restates what the group header already said.
  • Band color applied twice per audience (dot+word, then the colored lead-in sentence).
  • Good bones exist: the Lever rows and who-not-for lines are the honest payoff — promote them.
  • At 390px it runs 1,651px of dense text — nobody reads this on a phone.
Re-concept, not restyle: verdict card up top (hero = the Lever), room demoted to the ONE disclosure, quotes attributed once by grouping — the People-view "verdict-as-structure" trick (#339) already solved this shape.

Cross-cutting concept debts

Present even in language A
  1. The grammar has no "run" concept. Three hook cards = three identical action bars, three "See the room →" doors, three Saves, the audience named three times. Every card is self-contained because a card is the only unit that exists. The group states nothing; every row states everything.
  2. Band color multiplies. One card can apply its band tone to a dot, a word, a lead-in sentence, and a filled bar. The rule (once: dot + word) is written down but not structural — nothing prevents the fourth application.
  3. Receipt template tokens read as bugs. [common habit] / [timeframe] brackets are deliberate (the structure, abstracted) but visually indistinguishable from unfilled variables. First-glance read: "the app broke."
  4. Mono-caps ladders keep re-growing. The failure mode the contract names ("stacked equal-weight ALL-CAPS labels") was fixed in five cards and grew back in Explore's stat rows and the Text Read's attribution wall. Policing doesn't scale; only a closed vocabulary does.
  5. Mobile is an afterthought for data-heavy cards. Language A holds at 390px; B and C balloon to 4–5 screens. Density has no grammar-level answer today (no collapse rules, no priority order).
  6. 17 bespoke renderers is the root cost. Every new skill pays schema + renderer + fixtures + guard coverage. That price is why old cards rot: nobody re-pays it for surfaces that already "work."

What this means for the rework

The audit supports the grammar direction, with one big caveat to settle first. The composition idea (closed vocabulary of primitives, runners compose deterministically, chat composes ad-hoc) is exactly the shape that turns debts 01–06 from policy into structure: a run-group primitive kills the repetition, a band mark that can only render once kills the color multiplication, a closed set kills the ladder regrowth, per-primitive collapse rules give mobile an answer, and "new skill = a composition" kills the bespoke-renderer tax.

The caveat: a grammar codifies a language. Language A is what we'd codify — and you said you're not fully on board with the current concept. So the sequence matters: do we bless language A as the voice (and extract the vocabulary from it), or do we run a concept pass on the card language itself first — hero treatment, receipt look, proof unit, density — and codify the winner? Codifying first and re-concepting later means migrating everything twice.

Everything else from the scope discussion stands: hybrid composer (locked), Explore / Account / Text Read as the first three compositions (they're the proof the grammar can express hard cases), Reading page and Room out of scope.
